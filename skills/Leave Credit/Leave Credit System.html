Leave Credit System

An automated leave tracking engine that calculates leave credits from biometric attendance data — fairly, accurately, and without manual intervention.

◆ Technical Report ◆ 2026 ◆ Head Office / Rohtak / Bhiwadi
01

Introduction

The Leave Credit System automates the calculation of leave balances for employees based on their monthly biometric attendance records. It replaces error-prone manual tracking with a rule-based engine that consistently applies company policy.

The core principle is straightforward: employees earn leave credits when they work on holidays or weekly offs, and spend credits when they take leave on regular working days. The system tracks both sides of this equation and reports the net balance per employee.

02

Problem Statement

In attendance management, leave credits naturally flow in two directions:

＋

Credit Earned

Employee works on a Weekly Off or Company Holiday. Extra effort is rewarded with a future leave day.

+1 Credit
−

Credit Used

Employee takes leave (marked as COMP OFF or Absent in the attendance sheet) on a regular working day. A previously earned credit is consumed.

−1 Credit

Without automation, HR teams manually cross-reference holiday calendars, weekly off schedules, and attendance logs — a process that is slow, inconsistent, and prone to disputes. This system eliminates that overhead entirely.

03

Input Format

The system accepts an Excel (.xlsx) or CSV attendance file exported from any standard biometric attendance system. The expected columns are:

Column Description
Employee Code Unique identifier for each employee
Employee Name Full name of the employee
Date Date of attendance record (DD-MM-YYYY or YYYY-MM-DD)
In Time / Out Time Clock-in and clock-out timestamps
Working Hours Total hours worked (used to detect actual presence)
Status Attendance status — Present, Weekly Off, COMP OFF, Absent, etc.
Auto-detection: The system automatically locates the header row even if it isn't the first row. It also handles both row-per-day and transposed (dates-as-columns) file layouts.

Status Values & Impact

Each attendance record carries a status that determines its credit impact. The system recognizes multiple aliases for each status type:

P / Present
Normal working day, employee present
No change
WO / Weekly Off
Paid weekly rest day
No change*
CH / Holiday
Company-declared holiday
No change*
Present on WO
Employee worked on a weekly off
+1 Credit
Present on CH
Employee worked on a company holiday
+1 Credit
COMP OFF / Absent
Employee on leave (marked as COMP OFF or Absent in sheet)
−1 Credit
Key rule: Both Absent and COMP OFF are status values in the attendance sheet that indicate the employee took leave. They are treated identically. A deduction of −1 only applies if the day is a working day (not a Weekly Off or Company Holiday).

06

Processing Logic

The engine processes each attendance record through a six-step pipeline:

1

Load Attendance File

Read the Excel/CSV file, auto-detect the header row, normalize column names, and parse dates into a standard format.

2

Classify Each Day

For every attendance row, determine if the date is a Company Holiday, a Weekly Off (from status), or a regular Working Day.

3

Detect Credits Earned (+1)

If the day is a Weekly Off or Company Holiday AND the employee has non-zero working hours or a "Present" status, award +1 credit.

4

Detect Credits Used (−1)

If the status is COMP OFF or Absent (both indicate leave in the sheet) AND the day is a regular working day (not WO or CH), deduct −1 credit.

5

Aggregate Per Employee

Group all records by Employee Code + Name. Sum earned credits and used credits separately for each employee.

6

Generate Report

Output a formatted Excel file with a summary sheet and an optional day-by-day detail sheet.

07

Credit Formula

The final leave credit balance is computed per employee using a simple additive formula:

Formula
Leave Credits Left = Credits Earned − Credits Used

Where:

Credits Earned
Total days the employee worked on Weekly Offs or Company Holidays during the period.
Credits Used
Total days the employee took leave (marked as COMP OFF or Absent in the sheet) on regular working days.
Example
Employee worked 14 off-days − took 12 leaves = 2 credits remaining
Negative balances: If credits used exceed credits earned, the balance goes negative. The report highlights negative balances in red to flag employees who have overdrawn their leave entitlement.
08

Edge Cases

The system handles all common edge cases to ensure reliable output:

🛡️ Leave Taken on a Holiday or Weekly Off

⌄

If an employee's status shows COMP OFF or Absent but the date is a Company Holiday or Weekly Off, no deduction is applied. The day is already a non-working day — the leave status is treated as redundant.

📅 Absent on a Holiday or Weekly Off

⌄

Same treatment as above — no deduction. Being on leave on a day that's already an off-day carries no credit penalty.

🔄 Company Holiday + Weekly Off on Same Day

⌄

Both flags are set, but the day is still counted as a single off-day. If the employee worked, they earn +1 credit (not +2). The system avoids double-counting.

⚠️ Unparseable Dates or Missing Data

⌄

Rows with unparseable dates are skipped. Rows with missing Employee Code or Name are grouped under "UNKNOWN" and flagged in the report. Total/summary rows in the attendance file are automatically excluded.

📊 Multi-Sheet or Multi-Month Files

⌄

If the attendance file contains multiple sheets, all are processed. Records are deduplicated by Employee Code + Date to prevent double-counting across overlapping sheets.

09

Output Report

The system generates a professionally formatted Excel report with two sheets:

Sheet 1 — Leave Credits Summary

Column Description
Employee Code Unique identifier from attendance data
Employee Name Full name from attendance data
Credits Earned Count of days worked on WO/Holiday +1 each
Credits Used Count of Comp Off / Absent on working days −1 each
Leave Credits Left Earned − Used. Green if ≥ 0, red if negative.

Sheet 2 — Attendance Detail

A row-by-row breakdown showing each date, the original status, whether it was a holiday or weekly off, the credit change (+1 / −1 / 0), and the reason for any change. This provides full audit transparency.

Formatting: The report uses Arial 11pt throughout, bold headers with light blue fill, auto-fit column widths, and conditional coloring — green for positive balances, red for negative or overdrawn balances.

Conclusion

The Leave Credit System transforms a tedious, error-prone manual process into an automated, auditable, and policy-consistent workflow. By codifying the rules for credit earning and spending into a deterministic engine, it removes ambiguity from comp-off tracking.

Every decision the system makes — whether to award a credit, deduct one, or skip a row entirely — follows documented rules and is visible in the Attendance Detail sheet. This transparency eliminates disputes and builds trust between HR and employees.

The result is cleaner attendance records, faster month-end processing, and a reliable leave balance that both HR teams and employees can count on.
